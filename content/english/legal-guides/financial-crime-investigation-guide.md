# Responding to Financial Crime Investigations in China: A Practical Guide for High-Net-Worth Individuals

*Last updated: January 2026 | Reading time: 12 minutes*

Being the subject of a financial crime investigation in China is a serious matter that requires immediate, strategic action. This guide provides practical advice for high-net-worth individuals who are, or may become, subjects of investigation.

## Understanding the Investigation Process

Financial crime investigations in China may originate from multiple sources and proceed through several stages before reaching trial. Understanding this process is essential for effective response.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. How do financial crime investigations begin in China?

**Investigation Sources**:

**Regulatory Referral** (监管移送):
- CSRC detects market manipulation or insider trading through surveillance systems
- PBOC Anti-Money Laundering Center flags suspicious transactions
- Banking and insurance regulators identify compliance violations
- Tax authorities discover evasion through audit or data analytics

**Supervisory Commission** (监察委员会):
- Investigates corruption and bribery involving state functionaries
- Can investigate private individuals involved in bribery of officials
- Detention powers (留置) without judicial approval for up to 3 months

**Public Security Economic Crime Unit** (公安经侦部门):
- Receives complaints from victims or other parties
- Conducts preliminary investigation (初查) before formal opening
- Can initiate based on media reports or social media exposure

### 2. What are the early warning signs of an investigation?

Potential indicators:

- **Bank inquiries**: Banks requesting additional documentation or explanation of transactions
- **Regulatory notices**: CSRC, PBOC, or tax bureau information requests
- **Account freezing**: Partial or complete freezing of bank accounts
- **Travel restrictions**: Being informed you cannot leave the country
- **Witness inquiries**: Associates or business partners being questioned
- **Document requests**: Formal or informal requests for business records
- **Employee interviews**: Company employees being questioned by authorities

### 3. What should you do in the first 24 hours of learning about an investigation?

**Critical First Steps**:

1. **Engage a qualified criminal defense lawyer immediately**
   - A Shenzhen lawyer with financial crime experience is essential
   - Attorney-client privilege protects these communications
   - Early advice can prevent missteps that worsen the situation

2. **Preserve all evidence**
   - Do not delete emails, messages, or documents
   - Do not alter financial records
   - Destruction of evidence constitutes a separate crime (帮助毁灭证据罪)

3. **Do not contact potential witnesses**
   - Any communication could be construed as obstruction
   - Do not discuss the investigation with business partners
   - Do not attempt to coordinate stories

4. **Prepare a factual timeline**
   - Document your recollection while fresh
   - Share only with your attorney
   - This helps prepare for interviews and identify issues

### 4. What are the rights during interrogation?

Under the **Criminal Procedure Law** (《刑事诉讼法》):

- **Right to counsel**: Lawyer present during interrogation (since 2012 amendment)
- **Right to rest**: Interrogation cannot exceed 12 hours (24 for major cases)
- **Right to food and rest**: Must be provided during detention
- **Right against forced confession**: Torture and coercion are prohibited (Art. 52)
- **Right to review transcript**: Must review and sign interrogation records
- **Right to request corrections**: Can add or modify statements before signing

### 5. How should asset freezing be handled?

When assets are frozen during investigation:

**Legal Framework**:
- Courts and prosecutors can freeze assets suspected to be proceeds of crime
- Freezing should be limited to assets related to the investigation
- Duration: up to 6 months per freeze order (renewable)
- Frozen assets include bank accounts, securities, real estate, and vehicles

**Response Strategy**:
- Lawyer can challenge overbroad freezing orders
- Essential living expenses should be exempted
- Legitimate business operations should not be paralyzed
- Third-party rights (spouses, business partners) can be asserted
- Application for partial release of frozen assets possible

### 6. What is the process from investigation to trial?

**Typical Timeline**:

| Stage | Authority | Duration |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Preliminary investigation | Supervisory Commission / Public Security | Variable |
| Criminal detention (刑事拘留) | Public Security | Up to 37 days |
| Arrest approval (批准逮捕) | Procuratorate | 7 days from request |
| Investigation detention (侦查羁押) | Public Security | 2-7 months |
| Prosecution review (审查起诉) | Procuratorate | 1-6.5 months |
| Trial (审判) | Court | 2-6 months |

**Key Decision Points**:
- Whether to grant bail (取保候审) after arrest
- Whether to indict (起诉) after prosecution review
- Whether to offer plea agreement (认罪认罚)

### 7. How does bail (取保候审) work in financial crime cases?

**Eligibility Criteria**:
- Sentence likely to be less than public surveillance, criminal detention, or independent supplementary punishment
- Sentence likely to exceed fixed-term imprisonment but bail poses no social danger
- Serious illness, inability to care for oneself, pregnant, or nursing
- Detention period expired without case concluded

**Practical Considerations for HNWIs**:
- Bail amount typically RMB 100,000-1,000,000 for financial crimes
- Surrender of passport required
- Regular reporting to local police station
- Travel restrictions within and outside the city
- A lawyer's application for bail is taken more seriously than self-application

### 8. What post-investigation compliance measures should be implemented?

After resolution of an investigation:

- **Compliance program**: Establish or strengthen internal compliance
- **Monitoring**: Implement transaction monitoring systems
- **Training**: Regular legal compliance training for all staff
- **Documentation**: Maintain complete records of all financial transactions
- **Professional relationships**: Maintain ongoing relationship with defense counsel
- **Insurance**: Review and update D&O and professional liability coverage
