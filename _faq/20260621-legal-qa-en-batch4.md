### 1. What are the key compliance obligations for companies listed on Chinese stock exchanges?

**A:** Key obligations: 1) Timely disclosure of material information (within 2 business days); 2) Annual and quarterly financial reports; 3) Related party transaction disclosure and approval; 4) Insider trading prevention (maintain insider lists); 5) Corporate governance standards (independent directors, audit committees); 6) ESG disclosure (mandatory since 2024 for certain companies). Violations face CSRC administrative penalties, stock exchange disciplinary actions, and potential criminal liability.

### 2. How does China's export control system work?

**A:** China's Export Control Law (2020) establishes: 1) Controlled items list (military, nuclear, dual-use, and other national security items); 2) Licensing system administered by MOFCOM; 3) End-user and end-use certificates required; 4) Catch-all control for items not on the list but potentially used for WMD; 5) Re-export restrictions. Violations face fines up to 20x illegal revenue, business license revocation, and criminal prosecution. Companies should implement internal compliance programs.

### 3. What is the typical timeline and cost of commercial litigation in China?

**A:** Timeline: First instance 6 months (can be extended); Appeal 3 months; Enforcement varies. Simple cases may use summary procedure (3 months). Cost: Court fees based on claim amount (0.5-5% sliding scale); Attorney fees vary (typically 5-15% of claim). Advantages vs arbitration: lower cost, discovery process, precedent value. Disadvantages: longer timeline, local protectionism concerns, limited enforcement abroad. Choice depends on case specifics and enforcement needs.

### 4. What severance rights do foreign employees have when working in China?

**A:** Foreign employees with valid work permits enjoy the same labor law protections as Chinese citizens: 1) Economic compensation (N) for employer-initiated termination; 2) 2N for illegal termination; 3) Social insurance coverage (subject to bilateral agreements); 4) Work injury benefits; 5) Annual leave (5-15 days based on years of service). Key difference: termination may also require notification to immigration authorities and work permit cancellation.

### 5. What are the penalties for violating China's Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL)?

**A:** PIPL penalties (effective Nov 2021): 1) For serious violations: up to 50 million RMB or 5% of prior year's revenue; 2) Business suspension or license revocation; 3) Personal liability for responsible individuals (100K-1M RMB fines, employment bans); 4) For general violations: up to 10 million RMB or 2% of revenue. Additionally, procuratorates can file public interest lawsuits. Cross-border transfer violations are treated as particularly serious. Compliance requires: DPO appointment, impact assessments, and proper legal basis for processing.
