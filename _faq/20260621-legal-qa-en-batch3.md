## Intellectual Property

### 1. How to protect intellectual property rights when entering the Chinese market?

**A：** IP protection strategy in China: 1) Register trademarks early - China follows first-to-file system; 2) File patents for core technology before public disclosure; 3) Register copyrights for software and creative works; 4) Implement trade secret protection measures (NDAs, access controls); 5) Record IP with customs for border enforcement; 6) Monitor for infringement regularly and enforce promptly. China has strengthened IP courts and increased statutory damages up to 5 million RMB.

### 2. What are the key differences between Chinese and Western patent systems?

**A：** Key differences: 1) First-to-file system (not first-to-invent); 2) Utility model patents available (less examination, 10-year term); 3) Design patents separate from invention patents; 4) Software patents allowed if tied to technical solution; 5) Pharmaceutical patent linkage system (since 2021); 6) Patent term extension for pharma patents (up to 5 years); 7) Grace period of 6 months for certain disclosures. Filing in both Chinese and English is recommended for comprehensive protection.

## Consumer Rights

### 3. What consumer protection rights exist for online purchases in China?

**A：** Consumer Protection Law provides: 1) 7-day no-reason return for online purchases (Art.25); 2) Triple damages for fraud (Art.55, minimum 500 RMB); 3) Ten-fold damages for food safety violations (Food Safety Law Art.148); 4) Platform liability for failing to provide seller information; 5) Joint liability for advertising endorsers (for health-related products). Enforcement: 12315 hotline, online complaint platform, consumer association mediation, or court action.
