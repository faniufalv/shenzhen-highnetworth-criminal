## Securities Crime Defense

### 1. What are the sentencing standards for insider trading in China?

**Answer:** Under Article 180 of PRC Criminal Law: serious cases face up to 5 years imprisonment plus 1-5x illegal gains as fine; especially serious cases face 5-10 years. Prosecution threshold: trading volume over 500,000 RMB or illegal gains/avoided losses over 150,000 RMB. Defense points: information not legally defined as insider information, trading based on public research, transaction timing inconsistent with information formation.

---

### 2. How to distinguish market manipulation from legitimate investment in China?

**Answer:** Article 182 of Criminal Law covers continuous trading manipulation, matched orders, wash trades, and scalping manipulation. Key distinction: subjective intent to influence prices, use of capital/shareholding advantages, abnormal trading patterns. Simply having a bullish view and purchasing large amounts does not constitute manipulation.

---

### 3. What is the enterprise compliance non-prosecution system in China?

**Answer:** Per Supreme People's Procuratorate guidelines: companies accused of minor crimes can commit to compliance rectification during a 3-12 month review period. Upon successful third-party evaluation, non-prosecution may be granted. Requirements: minor offense (under 3 years sentence), normal business operations, voluntary guilty plea, willingness and capacity for reform.

---

### 4. What are the defense strategies for fundraising fraud cases in China?

**Answer:** Key defense: distinguish between illegal absorption of public deposits (Article 176) vs fundraising fraud (Article 192). The critical difference is 'illegal possession intent'. Defense points: funds were used for legitimate business operations, there was genuine intention to repay, no fabricated projects or false guarantees. Early voluntary surrender and restitution can significantly reduce sentencing.

---

### 5. How does China handle cross-border securities violations?

**Answer:** China's Securities Law Article 2 applies extraterritorially to activities that disrupt domestic markets. CSRC can cooperate with foreign regulators through IOSCO mechanisms. Defense considerations: jurisdictional challenges, conflict of laws, dual criminality requirements. Chinese law firms with international expertise are essential for such cases.
